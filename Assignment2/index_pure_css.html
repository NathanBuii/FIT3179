The Japanese Domestic Market (JDM) Car Scene in USA, Seen Through Craigslist

With the massive impact of The Fast and Furious and Initial D franchises in the 90s, the tuner scene in the USA exploded in popularity. Legendary cars like the Nissan Skyline, Toyota Supra and Mazda RX-7 are coveted for their aesthetic and build potential around the world. This visualisation will explore the JDM car market within the USA as shown through over 200,000 Craigslist used car listings (pre September 2020).

What's the Distribution of JDM Cars in the USA?

JDM cars make up just under 28% of the Craigslist market. By shear force of numbers, every US state contains more Non-JDM listings than JDM listings, but the proportion of JDM vs. Non-JDM doesn't always match the overall proportion. Most states follow Michigan and Wisconsin, with ~30% of listings being JDM cars, whereas more populous states like California, New York and Florida hover around 40-50%.

Do JDM Cars Age Well?

'Salvage' title cars have usually been significantly damaged, and deemed a total loss by the owner's insurance company. Car condition is as specified by the seller, not guaranteed to be the opinion of a professional mechanic.

Again by sheer force of numbers, Non-JDM cars vary much more in their condition to price. The strong majority of car listings are more expensive the newer they are, regardless of condition or market. However, there is an interesting upwards trend in the 70s-80s as those childhood favourite cars become 'classics'. Interestingly, there is a pocket of 'Good' condition cars from 2010-2020 which are priced below $1,000 USD, either as initial auction listings or typos.
